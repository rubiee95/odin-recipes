Taiwanese Beef Noodle Soup

Taiwanese beef noodle soup is the ultimate comfort food to keep you warm during winter. Youâ€™ll love the tender beef stewed for hours in the accompanying savory, full-bodied broth. Traditionally, Taiwanese beef noodle soup is enjoyed with a handful of fresh greens, chopped cilantro, pickled mustard greens, and chili oil. This recipe makes a big batch. So, get ready to invite friends over or save the beef noodle soup for belly-warming leftovers.

Ingredients

  • 100g beef shank, cut into strips
  • 4-5 slices of ginger
  • 1 medium onion, cut into slices
  • 2 green shallot or green onion
  • 10 cloves of garlic
  • 90-100g soy sauce
  • 2Tbsp rock sugar
  • 3.5Tbsp spicy bean paste
  • 3Tbsp rice wine
  • 2 star anise
  • 2 bay leaf
  • 2tsp chinese red pepper flakes
  • 1 whole tomato

Steps

  1. Add some oil into a medium pot. Saute ginger and garlic on low-medium heat until golden brown.
  2. Add green onions and beef. Saute until surface of the beef is cooked.
  3. In a larger soup pot, saute onions until translucent. Add in soy sauce, sugar, been paste, rice wine, anise, bay leaf, chinese red pepper. Put seasonings in a tea bag.
  4. Add beef into large pot and add water until the beef is submerged. After boiling on high, cover and turn to low heat. Simmer for 2-3 hours
  5. Serve with cooked noodles and bok choy.
